Thesis Research

Junior Investigator, Graduate Student Researcher, Genetics, Biomedical Data Science

My research includes exercise multiomics and creating single-cell spatial profiles with computational/systems cancer biology, genetics, genomics, and bioinformatics. Specifically, I’m creating an exercise interorgan crosstalking map that has single-cell resolution spatial representations from public datasets spatial transcriptomics, spatial protoemics, multiplex imaging (e.g., IHC, mIF), and navigate cellular atlas roadmaps routes towards multicellular neighborhoods, communities, and direct spatial context maps in disease and ligand-receptor interactions that measure exerkine, ligand, and receptor molecular transducers. This novel research on exerkines in health, human disease, and resilience in multiple research/scientific/engineering communities (i.e., consortia): Molecular Transducers of Physical Activity (MoTrPAC), Genotype-Tissue Expression (GTEx) project, Human Biomolecular Atlas Project (HuBMAP), Human Tumor Atlas Network (HTAN), Human Cell Atlas, Human Gut Cell Atlas.
